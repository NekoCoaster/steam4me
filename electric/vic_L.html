L Class Electric Locomotives (VIC) for MSTS

original 3d model design and textures by Geoff Forrest

Overview

Package contains twenty-three different locomotives:

Victorian Railways (blue and gold)

  1. L1163 - Silver/black bogies as delivered, pre-mid 50s era , no number boards, rear pantograph raised
  2. L1166 - Silver/black bogies as delivered pre-mid 50s era, no number boards, front pantograph raised
  3. L1150 - Named "R G Wishart", number boards, 50s-70s, front pantograph raised
  4. L1151 - As in service 50s-70s rear pantograph raised
  5. L1152 - As in service 50s-70s rear pantograph raised
  6. L1153 - As in service 70s-80s, dead in train, both pantographs lowered
  7. L1155 - As in service 70s-80s, staff exchanger removed, front pantograph raised, Beclawat* driver's window
  8. L1158 - As in service 70s-80s, staff exchanger removed, rear pantograph raised, Beclawat* driver's window
  9. L1161 - As in service 70s-80s, staff exchanger removed, dual marker lights, front Faiveley pantograph raised
  10. L1162 - As preserved 2000s
  11. L1165 - As in service 70s-80s, staff exchanger removed, single front pantograph
  12. L1167 - As in service 70s-80s, staff exchanger removed, Faiveley front pantograph
  13. L1171 - As in service 1976-1986, modified nose profile (only locomotive in class thus converted)
  14. L1172 - As in service 70s-80s, staff exchanger removed, single rear pantograph
  15. L1173 - As in service 70s-80s, staff exchanger removed, nose-mounted horns

    16. VicRail "Teacup" Livery

  16. L1150 - As in service 1981-83, named "R G Wishart" (Unique - only L ever painted into this livery)

    V/Line "experimental" tangerine livery

  17. L1156 - As in service 1983 (Unique - only L ever painted into this livery)

    V/Line grey and orange

  18. L1150 - As in service 1980s
  19. L1159 - As in service 1980s
  20. L1160 - As in service 1980s
  21. L1162 - As in service 1980s
  22. L1169 - As in service 1980s, dead in train, both pantographs lowered
  23. L1174 - As in service 1980s

* Beclawat: Originally Beckett, Laycock and Watkinson of Acton, England, Beclawat is now a Canadian company manufacturing windows and doors for the transport industry.
